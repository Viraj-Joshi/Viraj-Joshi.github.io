## What did you like the least about the class?

I would like to preface this before saying anything else that this is the first CS class I've taken that actually made me want to understand everything. 

Although when I signed up for the class, I thought I would be learning about inheritance, polymorphism, and abstractions for a majority of the course rather than the last few weeks. Secondly, we used a lot of tools like cppcheck, astyle, doxygen,etc and were told these were industry tools but never anymore than that. Lastly, the blogs felt tedious since the course does not have a writing flag. 

## What did you like the most about the class?

I wanted to take a course using C++ because I wanted to be able to HackerRanks in C++ for the fall recruiting season. I think I can do that now.

## What's the most significant thing you learned?

++i vs i++, just kidding, most likely developing an intuition to writing solutions to algorithms problems in C++ just as well as I could in Java. We also learned a lot of copy constructors and copy assignment.

## How many hours a week did you spend coding/debugging/testing for this class?

It varies week to week. I really only worked on projects for 5 days with about 3-4 hours per day with a partner. The exceptions was the last project which was more like 18 hours in total. 

Other times, I spent maybe 15 minutes before every lecture reviewing notes. 

## How many hours a week did you spend reading/studying for this class?

It varies week to week.  For weeks with tests, 10 hours doing HackerRanks and trying to reconcile Piazza notes, GitLab notes, and my notes. The Perusuall articles were sometimes useful, but usually took about 30 minutes to do.
